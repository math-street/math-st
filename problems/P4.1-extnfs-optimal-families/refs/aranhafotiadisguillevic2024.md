# Aranha–Fotiadis–Guillevic 2024

[CITED] Diego F. Aranha, Georgios Fotiadis, and Aurore Guillevic, “A Short-List of Pairing-Friendly Curves Resistant to the Special TNFS Algorithm at the 192-bit Security Level,” *IACR Communications in Cryptology* 1(3), article 3, 2024, doi:10.62056/angyl86bm.

## Main result in repository notation

[CITED] The paper evaluates complete families with embedding degrees 15 through 28 alongside established BN, BLS, and KSS baselines, and combines TNFS estimates with implementation-oriented operation counts.

[CITED] Its baseline table lists concrete 192-bit seeds for BN, BLS12, KSS16, KSS18, and BLS24, including base-field sizes 1022 bits for BN and 509 bits for BLS24.

## Relevance

[CITED] The paper verifies that the current practical selection problem uses a wider family set and multiple objectives, so it is not equivalent to the four-family minimum-$\rho$ experiment here.
