# Kim–Barbulescu 2016

[CITED] Taechan Kim and Razvan Barbulescu, “Extended Tower Number Field Sieve: A New Complexity for the Medium Prime Case,” CRYPTO 2016 Part I, LNCS 9814, 543–571, doi:10.1007/978-3-662-53018-4_20; IACR ePrint 2015/1027.

## Main result in repository notation

[CITED] If a polynomial-selection method attacks $\mathbb F_{P^\kappa}$, exTNFS transfers it to $\mathbb F_{p^{\eta\kappa}}$ when $P$ has the size of $p^\eta$ and the required tower conditions hold; the paper derives improved $L$-notation constants for relevant composite extension degrees (§1 and Theorem 1).

## Assumptions used here

[CITED] The asymptotic $L$ cost suppresses an $o(1)$ term and does not determine finite-size sieving constants (§1).

## What it leaves open

[CITED] The asymptotic result alone does not supply the family-specific norm simulations needed for concrete key-size estimates; later finite-size work performs that optimization.
