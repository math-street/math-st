# SIKE specification 2022

SIKE team, *Supersingular Isogeny Key Encapsulation*, NIST Round-4 submission
specification, 2022-09-15.

## Protocol fact used by P3.4

[CITED] An uncompressed public key stores the $x$-coordinates of the images of
$P,Q,P-Q$ under the secret isogeny, from which the public codomain curve and the
needed torsion-map encoding are obtained (Sections 1.2--1.3).

[CITED] Public-key compression represents equivalent full torsion-basis data
rather than deleting the restriction (Section 1.5).

[CITED] The system parameters expose the 2-power and 3-power isogeny degrees,
and their factorizations are immediate (Sections 1.1 and 1.6).

[CITED] The postscript says SIKE and SIDH are insecure and records the 2022
Castryck--Decru attack and follow-up work (Chapter 0).
