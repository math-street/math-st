# Lenstra--Pila--Pomerance smoothness testing

[CITED] Hendrik W. Lenstra Jr., Jonathan Pila, and Carl Pomerance, “A Hyperelliptic Smoothness Test, I,” *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society A* 345 (1993), pp. 397--408, DOI 10.1098/rsta.1993.0138.

[CITED] Primary source checked: <https://math.dartmouth.edu/~carlp/hyperI.pdf>.

[CITED] Part II is “A Hyperelliptic Smoothness Test, II,” *Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society* 84 (2002), pp. 105--146, DOI 10.1112/plms/84.1.105.

[CITED] The results give rigorous expected-time machinery for finding small factors; Lee--Venkatesan's Fact 3.18 and Corollary 3.19 state the consequence that a $y$-smooth integer up to $x$ can be factored in $y^{o(1)}$ expected time when $y=(\log x)^{\omega(1)}$.

[PROVED] This discharges the accepted-candidate factorization-cost issue in the exTNFS asymptotic range, but not the supply of smooth norm values.
