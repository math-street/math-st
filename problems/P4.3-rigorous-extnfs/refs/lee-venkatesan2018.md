# Lee--Venkatesan 2018

[CITED] E. Lee and Ramarathnam Venkatesan, “Rigorous Analysis of a Randomised Number Field Sieve,” *Journal of Number Theory* 187 (2018), pp. 92--159, DOI 10.1016/j.jnt.2017.10.019; arXiv:1805.08873.

[CITED] Primary source checked: <https://arxiv.org/pdf/1805.08873>.

[CITED] Theorem 2.1 gives expected $L_n(1/3,(64/9)^{1/3}+o(1))$ time to find a congruence of squares in the randomized ordinary NFS.  Theorem 2.3 conditions the conclusion that this congruence factors $n$ on the paper's character Conjecture 7.1.

[CITED] Theorem 2.5 proves simultaneous smooth relation generation by randomizing the defining polynomial so that evaluation at a fixed pair varies in an arithmetic progression, then applying smooth-number results in progressions and stochastic deepening.

[CITED] Fact 3.18 and Corollary 3.19 invoke hyperelliptic smoothness testing to rigorously factor accepted smooth candidates in subpolynomial-in-the-bound expected time.

[PROVED] In a tower, the extra outer field norm is nonlinear when $\eta>1$; therefore the arithmetic-progression argument does not directly imply smoothness of the resulting integer norm.
