# Kim--Barbulescu 2016

[CITED] Taechan Kim and Razvan Barbulescu, “Extended Tower Number Field Sieve: A New Complexity for the Medium Prime Case,” CRYPTO 2016, LNCS 9814, pp. 543--571, DOI 10.1007/978-3-662-53018-4_20; IACR ePrint 2015/1027.

[CITED] Primary source checked: <https://eprint.iacr.org/2015/1027.pdf>.

[CITED] Relevant content: Sections 2--4 define the tower, the iterated-resultant norms, two-sided relation collection, factor bases, and sparse linear algebra.  The complexity calculation explicitly treats each norm as smooth with the probability of an arbitrary integer of the same size and multiplies the two probabilities.

[CITED] Relevant content: the paper's theorems state the exTNFS complexities under “classical NFS heuristics.”  Its best Conjugation constant is $(48/9)^{1/3}$; JLSV2 gives $(64/9)^{1/3}$ in the stated ranges.

[CITED] Relevant content: the individual-log appendices use randomized target lifts, smoothness and squarefreeness tests, and a special-$q$ lattice descent whose local success probability is modeled with the Dickman function.  The Conjugation polynomial construction also labels its irreducibility trial count as heuristic.
