# Bender--Pomerance 1998

[CITED] Renet Lovorn Bender and Carl Pomerance, “Rigorous Discrete Logarithm Computations in Finite Fields via Smooth Polynomials,” *AMS/IP Studies in Advanced Mathematics* 7 (1998), pp. 221--232.

[CITED] Primary source checked: <https://math.dartmouth.edu/~carlp/PDF/paper115.pdf>.

[CITED] The paper gives a rigorous index-calculus algorithm for every finite field $\mathbb F_{p^n}$ using smooth polynomials.  Its growing-extension bounds include $L_Q(1/2,\sqrt2+o(1))$ when $p\le n^{o(n)}$, $L_Q(1/2,\sqrt{8/3}+o(1))$ in an intermediate range, and $p^{2+o(1)}$ when $p>n^{4n/3}$.

[CITED] Theorem 2.2 supplies the elementary uniform lower bound on the number of smooth monic polynomials that underpins relation generation.

[PROVED] The method supplies a rigorous DLP fallback for the problem's fields, but it is not exTNFS and does not give an $L_Q(1/3)$ bound.
