# Barbulescu--Lachand 2017

[CITED] Razvan Barbulescu and Sylvain Lachand, “Some Mathematical Remarks on Polynomial Selection in NFS,” *Mathematics of Computation* 86 (2017), pp. 397--418, DOI 10.1090/mcom/3112; arXiv:1403.0184.

[CITED] Primary source checked: <https://arxiv.org/pdf/1403.0184>.

[CITED] Theorem C gives a positive lower density of simultaneous smooth values for a finite set of fixed irreducible binary forms only in restricted dense-smoothness ranges.  It does not give the random-integer density for the varying tower forms used by exTNFS.

[CITED] Theorem 4.1 gives a Dickman-type asymptotic for smooth ideals of a fixed number field, uniformly over a broad smoothness range for that field.  It counts ideals rather than short principal generators shared by two NFS sides, and it does not supply uniformity as the field varies.

[CITED] Theorem 4.2 gives a more precise, alpha-corrected result for a fixed quadratic binary form.  Its fixed degree, discriminant, and region hypotheses exclude the exTNFS family.
