# Balog--Blomer--Dartyge--Tenenbaum 2012

[CITED] Antal Balog, Valentin Blomer, Cecile Dartyge, and Gerald Tenenbaum, “Friable Values of Binary Forms,” *Commentarii Mathematici Helvetici* 87 (2012), pp. 639--667, DOI 10.4171/CMH/264.

[CITED] Primary source: <https://ems.press/journals/cmh/articles/1819>.

[CITED] The paper proves positive-proportion friable-value results for fixed binary forms, including a general threshold of the shape $y\ge x^{g-2+\epsilon}$ for degree $g$ and improvements in small degrees.

[PROVED] Those hypotheses do not include the parameter-dependent, multivariate iterated-resultant forms or the sparse $L_Q(1/3)$ smoothness regime needed by exTNFS.
