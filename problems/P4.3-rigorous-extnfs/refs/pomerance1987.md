# Pomerance 1987

[CITED] Carl Pomerance, “Fast, Rigorous Factorization and Discrete Logarithm Algorithms,” in *Discrete Algorithms and Complexity*, Academic Press, 1987, pp. 119--143.

[CITED] Primary source checked: <https://math.dartmouth.edu/~carlp/disclog.pdf>.

[CITED] The paper rigorously analyzes randomized subexponential algorithms for factoring and for discrete logarithms when the field order is prime or a power of two.  It obtains preprocessing exponent $\sqrt2+o(1)$ and individual-log exponent $1/\sqrt2+o(1)$ in its $L(q)=\exp(\sqrt{\log q\log\log q})$ notation.

[PROVED] This is an important randomization precedent but does not cover odd-prime medium-characteristic extensions or tower norm forms.
