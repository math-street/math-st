# Remaud–Schrottenloher–Tillich 2022

- [CITED] Maxime Remaud, André Schrottenloher, and Jean-Pierre Tillich, “Time and Query Complexity Tradeoffs for the Dihedral Coset Problem,” arXiv:2206.14408 (2022).
- [CITED] The paper interpolates between logarithmic-query exponential-time and subexponential-query/subexponential-time regimes by combining Kuperberg-style preprocessing with subset-sum solvers.
- [CITED] Its CSIDH-512 examples include query exponents 19 for Regev's approach and 11 for one QRACM subset-sum tradeoff, paired with much larger classical or quantum time exponents.
- [PROVED] These examples reinforce that a query exponent alone is not a complete physical attack cost; the other resource columns are separate inputs.
