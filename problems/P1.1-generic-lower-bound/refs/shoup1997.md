# Shoup 1997

- [CITED] Theorem 1 bounds the success probability of a generic DLP algorithm
  making at most $m$ group-oracle queries by $O(m^2/r)$, where $r$ is the
  largest prime divisor of the group order.
- [CITED] The proof simulates group elements by affine-linear polynomials in a
  hidden logarithm and random opaque labels; accidental equality of distinct
  formal polynomials is the bad event.
- [CITED] The paper explicitly excludes algorithms that exploit properties of
  concrete encodings.

Primary source: Victor Shoup, EUROCRYPT 1997, revised PDF from the author's
publication page, `https://www.shoup.net/papers/dlbounds1.pdf`.
