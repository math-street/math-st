# Gaudry–Hess–Smart 2002

- [CITED] The paper studies Weil descent for elliptic curves over
  characteristic-two fields of composite degree.
- [CITED] The class-group homomorphism is conorm from the elliptic function
  field to the compositum, followed by norm to the fixed field.
- [CITED] Theorem 5 gives a hyperelliptic fixed field. In the odd-degree,
  magic-number-one case its equation is genus one, with the $x^2$ coefficient
  equal to the relative trace of the source coefficient.
- [CITED] The original KASH implementation returns the fixed field and mapped
  places, checks the same odd-degree trace equation, and records image degree
  $n2^{m-1}$; this becomes $n$ at $m=1$.

Primary source: Pierrick Gaudry, Florian Hess, and Nigel P. Smart,
“Constructive and Destructive Facets of Weil Descent on Elliptic Curves,”
*Journal of Cryptology* 15(1), 2002, pp. 19–46,
DOI `10.1007/s00145-001-0011-x`.
