# Wang et al. -- limitations of sparse Shoup GGM

Taiyu Wang, Cong Zhang, Hong-Sheng Zhou, Xin Wang, Pengfei Chen, Wenli Wang,
Kui Ren, and Chun Chen, “Attention is still what you need: Another Round of
Exploring Shoup's GGM,” ASIACRYPT 2025; IACR ePrint 2025/1930.

Primary record: <https://eprint.iacr.org/2025/1930>

## Relevant result

[CITED] The abstract identifies sparse encodings as the mechanism preventing
algorithms from generating valid labels without the group oracle, and gives
black-box separations showing that sparse GGM does not capture CDH-secure
groups with admissible encodings or CDH-secure dense groups.

[CITED] The abstract states that EC-GGM is strictly stronger than sparse GGM
under the paper's indifferentiability formulation and that the separations
extend to generic bilinear groups.
