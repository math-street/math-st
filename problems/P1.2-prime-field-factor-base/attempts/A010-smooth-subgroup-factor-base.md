# A010 — Smooth multiplicative-subgroup factor base

## Idea

[CITED] Petit–Kosters–Messeng 2016 propose factor-base abscissas in a smooth
multiplicative subgroup of $\mathbb F_p^*$, replacing one high-degree root
condition by a chain of low-degree power maps.

## Prior art

[CITED] The PKC 2016 paper reports prime-field Gröbner experiments but leaves
their asymptotic system-solving complexity open. Amadori–Pintore–Sala 2018
reduce a related attack to one system and likewise state that its complexity is
not understood well enough to beat Pollard rho.

## Plan

1. Use $p=65537$ and the unique subgroup $H\subset\mathbb F_p^*$ of order
   $64=2^6$.
2. Find a deterministic prime-order ordinary curve and define
   $\mathcal F=\{P:x(P)\in H\}$.
3. Exhaustively validate $x^{64}=1$ and the six repeated-squaring constraints.
4. Measure exact three-term decomposition counts and generic finder work
   against size-matched random bases on common targets.
5. Determine whether the low-degree coordinate description changes density or
   supplies an actual coordinate-aware finder.

## Outcome

[PROVED] Membership is succinct: because $\mathbb F_{65537}^{*}$ is cyclic,
the unique order-64 subgroup is exactly the root set of $x^{64}-1$; six
quadratic squaring equations evaluate this predicate using $O(\log 64)$
field operations.

[EMPIRICAL: $p=65537$, 96 targets] The subgroup base had mean decomposition
count 4.042 against exact random expectation 3.282 and success 0.510. Its
normalized-count ratio to matched random bases was 1.112 with 95% bootstrap
interval $[0.819,1.470]$.

[EMPIRICAL: same run] Its pair-check ratio to matched random bases was 0.969
with 95% bootstrap interval $[0.827,1.130]$. No factor-two generic-search
improvement was detected.

[EMPIRICAL: $p=17,257,65537$] At $p=17$ the direct system returned 7 basis
polynomials in 0.279 seconds and the chain returned 18 in 1.131 seconds. At
$p=257$ and $p=65537$ both encodings exceeded five seconds. At $p=65537$ the
chain reduced maximum input degree from 64 to 12 while increasing variables
from 3 to 18 and equations from 4 to 19.

[PROVED] A timeout is neither a proof that a target lacks a decomposition nor
a lower bound against another solver. [CITED] Petit–Kosters–Messeng 2016 and
Amadori–Pintore–Sala 2018 likewise leave the relevant system-solving
complexity open.

**Prediction vs. outcome:** [EMPIRICAL: preregistered $p=65537$ density run
and $p=17,257$ Gröbner run] Matched. The base size and predicate checks met
their controls, both confidence intervals met the preregistered null region,
and the $p=257$ systems timed out.

## Post-mortem

**Why it failed:** [EMPIRICAL: tested implementation through $p=65537$] The
factor-base predicate became low-degree and succinct, but neither generic
pair search nor the tested Gröbner solver became polynomial in $\log p$.
Consequently A010 does not provide the decoder required by Variant S.

**What transfers:** [PROVED] Smooth subgroup roots give an exact
coordinate-aware predicate outside A009's translate-probe model, and their
root condition admits a low-degree composition chain.

**Would it work under different assumptions?** [CONDITIONAL: a proved
polylogarithmic solver for the resulting summation-polynomial systems] The
same construction would become a Variant-S candidate after a separate total
resource and success-probability proof. No such solver is established here.
