# MIT 18.721 notes — Bézout's theorem

[CITED] Massachusetts Institute of Technology, *Notes for a Course in
Algebraic Geometry*, 18.721 preliminary draft, 11 December 2021, Sections 1.10
and 7.8. <https://math.mit.edu/classes/18.721/notes/ag-dec11-2021.pdf>

## Relevance

[CITED] Bézout's theorem bounds the number of geometric intersection points of
distinct degree-$m$ and degree-$n$ projective plane curves by $mn$ and counts
the intersection with multiplicity as exactly $mn$.

[PROVED] Applied to a plane cubic $E$ and a distinct degree-$d$ auxiliary
curve, this gives at most $3d$ candidate factor-base points.
