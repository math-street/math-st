# NIST 2013 — FIPS 186-4

**Reference.** [CITED] National Institute of Standards and Technology,
*Digital Signature Standard (DSS)*, FIPS PUB 186-4, July 2013,
doi:10.6028/NIST.FIPS.186-4.
<https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/FIPS/NIST.FIPS.186-4.pdf>

**Relevant source facts.** [CITED] Appendix D says its curves were generated
with SHA-1, publishes the P-256 160-bit seed and resulting parameters, and
describes the supplied base point as a sample while allowing any point of
order \(n\). Its prime-case algorithm chooses an arbitrary seed and then
chooses \(a,b\) satisfying the hash-derived relation \(cb^2=a^3\).

**Audit use. [PROVED]** The seed gives a 160-bit full-seed sensitivity cap.
After P-256 fixes \(a=-3\), the unresolved coefficient root adds at most one
core bit. The appendix does not give a deterministic origin for the seed.
