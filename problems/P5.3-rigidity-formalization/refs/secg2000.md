# SECG 2000 — SEC 2 version 1

**Reference. [CITED]** Standards for Efficient Cryptography Group,
*SEC 2: Recommended Elliptic Curve Domain Parameters*, version 1.0,
September 20, 2000. <https://www.secg.org/SEC2-Ver-1.0.pdf>

**Relevant source facts. [CITED]** Section 2.1 says the recommended
prime-field Koblitz parameters were chosen by repeatedly selecting parameters
admitting an efficiently computable endomorphism until a prime-order curve was
found. Section 2.7.1 publishes the secp256k1 tuple, including \(a=0\) and
\(b=7\), but gives no candidate domain, enumeration or sampling distribution,
rejected-candidate transcript, or generator derivation.

**Audit use. [PROVED]** This is stronger provenance evidence than the final
constants alone, but it does not determine the number of distinct screenable
histories. It therefore does not turn the A256 \(\bot\) entry into a numerical
capacity.
