# Herlédan Le Merdy and Wesolowski (2026 version)

Arthur Herlédan Le Merdy and Benjamin Wesolowski, “Unconditional foundations for supersingular isogeny-based cryptography,” TCC 2025, 266--297; arXiv:2502.17010v2, revised 2026-02-02.

- [CITED] Theorem 1 proves unconditional probabilistic-polynomial-time equivalence of unrestricted `Isogeny`, `EndRing`, `MaxOrder`, `HomModule`, `OneEnd`, and related variants.
- [CITED] Section 1.1 explicitly excludes $\ell$-`IsogenyPath` from that unconditional equivalence and says its reduction to `EndRing` remains GRH-conditional.
- [CITED] The proof avoids KLPT-type distribution arguments by using arbitrary-ideal-to-isogeny translation, flexible quaternion models, and a local quaternion/endomorphism dictionary.
- [CITED] Proposition 4 translates an arbitrary ideal into an efficiently represented isogeny, while Proposition 12 applies it to a connecting ideal and promises no prescribed smooth degree.
- [CITED] Proposition 13 constructs a flexible quaternion model and an explicit MOER dictionary directly from an endomorphism-ring basis, unconditionally.
- [PROVED] These results remove the special-curve dictionary use of D1, but they do not supply the small-discriminant special order required internally by Wesolowski's smooth-equivalent-ideal Algorithm 2; see A006.
- [CITED] Section 1.1 reports that a mistake was found in Mamah's 2024 factoring-oracle attempt.
- [CITED] Full 33-page v2 text checked on 2026-06-29.
