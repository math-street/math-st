# Bennett, Martin, O'Bryant, and Rechnitzer (2018)

- [CITED] Michael A. Bennett, Greg Martin, Kevin O'Bryant, and Andrew Rechnitzer, "Explicit bounds for primes in arithmetic progressions," *Illinois Journal of Mathematics* 62(1--4), 427--532 (2018); arXiv:1802.00085v3.
- [CITED] Theorems 1.1--1.3 give explicit error bounds for \(\psi(x;q,a)\), \(\theta(x;q,a)\), and \(\pi(x;q,a)\) for every coprime residue class and every modulus \(q\ge3\).
- [CITED] For \(q>10^5\), their uniform threshold is at most \(\exp(0.03\sqrt q\log^3q)\).
- [PROVED] With \(q=M=8\ell\), this gives the effective progression-prime density needed by A003 at output bit length polynomial in the numerical value of \(\ell\).
