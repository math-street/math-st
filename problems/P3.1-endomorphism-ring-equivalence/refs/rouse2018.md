# Rouse (2018)

- [CITED] Jeremy Rouse, "Integers represented by positive-definite quadratic forms and Petersson inner products," arXiv:1802.03437v1 (2018).
- [CITED] Theorem 1 gives representation crossovers for arbitrary primitive positive-definite quaternary forms in terms of their level and discriminant.
- [CITED] The strong-local-solubility, primitive-local-solubility, and general-local-solubility cases are effective; the sharper coprime case is ineffective because of a possible Siegel zero.
- [CITED] The proof decomposes the theta coefficient into Eisenstein and cusp parts, provides explicit local-density lower bounds, and bounds the cusp coefficient using the Petersson norm.
- [PROVED] The effective strong-local case is the relevant input for A003 because it avoids both fixed binary-form prime distribution and the ineffective coprime-case constant.
