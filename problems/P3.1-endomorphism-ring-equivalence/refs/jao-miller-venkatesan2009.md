# Jao, Miller, and Venkatesan (2009)

David Jao, Stephen D. Miller, and Ramarathnam Venkatesan, “Expander graphs based on GRH with an application to elliptic curve cryptography,” *Journal of Number Theory* 129(6), 1491--1504 (2009); arXiv:0811.0647v2; doi:10.1016/j.jnt.2008.11.006.

- [CONDITIONAL: GRH] Theorem 1.1 bounds nontrivial eigenvalues for ray-class Cayley graphs generated by prime ideals of norm at most $(\log q)^B$, $B>2$.
- [CONDITIONAL: GRH] Corollary 1.3 converts the spectral gap into logarithmic-length random-walk mixing.
- [CITED] Sections 1 and 7.2 explain that an all-modulus unconditional analogue meets the least-prime-quadratic-nonresidue obstruction; average-modulus results do not give the required worst-case theorem.
