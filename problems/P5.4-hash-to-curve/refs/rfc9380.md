# Faz-Hernandez et al. 2023 - RFC 9380

## Reference

- [CITED] A. Faz-Hernandez, S. Scott, N. Sullivan, R. S. Wahby, and C. A. Wood, *Hashing to Elliptic Curves*, RFC 9380, August 2023. <https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc9380.html>

## Results used here

- [CITED] Section 3 defines `hash_to_curve` as two independently derived field elements, two deterministic map evaluations, point addition, and cofactor clearing.
- [CITED] Sections 6.6.2 and F.2 specify direct SSWU and its straight-line implementation for $A B\ne0$.
- [CITED] Section 6.6.3 specifies the isogeny workaround for $AB=0$.
- [CITED] Sections 6.7.1 and F.3 specify Elligator 2 and its straight-line implementation for eligible Montgomery curves.
- [CITED] Section 6.8 transports Elligator 2 to twisted Edwards curves through a suite-fixed Montgomery equivalent and rational map.
- [CITED] Appendix D gives generic rational transports between Weierstrass, Montgomery, and twisted-Edwards presentations.
- [CITED] Section 5 specifies bias-controlled `hash_to_field` and SHA-family message expansion without rejection sampling.
- [CITED] Appendix K.1 supplies the SHA-256 XMD vectors used as official anchors in this repository.
- [CITED] Section 10.1 states the indifferentiability result for the full composition under the stated `hash_to_field` assumptions.

## What it does not establish for this repository

- [PROVED] The RFC does not make Python big-integer arithmetic constant-time; its low-level constant-time requirements are obligations on implementations.
- [PROVED] The RFC suite vectors use production-size fields and therefore cannot be executed under this repository's $\log_2q\le60$ experiment ceiling.
- [CITED] The RFC explicitly excludes elliptic curves over fields of characteristic two or three (Faz-Hernandez et al. 2023, Section 2.1).

## Session-1 discrepancy

- [PROVED] Appendix F.2.1.1 returns `isQR = False` when its numerator is zero, because every temporary entering the equality test is zero, while Section 4 defines zero as square. The concrete admissible fixture and full derivation are recorded in `NOTES.md`.
