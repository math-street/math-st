## Result relevant to P2.1

[CITED] Theorem 1 proves that there are infinitely many primes $r$ for which

$$
  P^+(r-1)>r^{0.679}.
$$

[PROVED] For every fixed $C$, all sufficiently large members of this infinite
family satisfy $P^+(r-1)>(\log r)^C$.  Hence the full multiplicative auxiliary
group $\mathbb F_r^*$ cannot satisfy Maurer's polylogarithmic smoothness
condition for every prime $r$.

[PROVED] The same family obstructs the full groups
$\mathbb F_{r^n}^*$ for every $n\ge1$, because $r-1$ divides $r^n-1$.
This divisibility argument says nothing about a specially selected subgroup
whose order omits that factor.
