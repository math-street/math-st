## Result used in P2.1

[CITED] Linnik's theorem gives effectively computable absolute constants
$C,L>0$ such that, for every coprime residue $a$ modulo $m$, the least prime
$p\equiv a\pmod m$ satisfies $p\le C m^L$.  Xylouris's Theorem 1.1 makes
$L=5.2$ admissible.

[PROVED] P2.1 only needs the existence of one absolute exponent.  The explicit
value converts a CRT modulus of size $O(X^2)$ into a prime of size
$O(X^{10.4})$, leaving both forced modulus factors polynomially large in that
prime.
