## Main result relevant to P2.1

[CITED] Theorem 1.2 guarantees a $y$-smooth number in every sufficiently large
interval of the stated form only when
$y\ge\exp(C(\log x)^{2/3}(\log\log x)^{4/3})$ and the interval length is at
least $\sqrt{x}$ multiplied by an explicit growing factor.

## What it rules out and leaves open

[PROVED] This result does not imply a polylogarithmically smooth integer in
the Hasse interval: its allowed $y$ is super-polylogarithmic and its required
all-interval length is asymptotically larger than $4\sqrt{x}$.

[CITED] Section 3 recalls the Dickman function and the standard global
estimate $\rho(u)=u^{-u+o(u)}$, which is used only as a heuristic baseline for
the P2.1 finite experiment.
