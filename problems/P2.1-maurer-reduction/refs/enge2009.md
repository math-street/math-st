1089--1107, 2009, arXiv:cs/0601104.

Primary text checked at:

## Result in P2.1 notation

[CITED] Section 2 states the CM construction: if a negative discriminant $D$
and integers $t,v$ satisfy $4r=t^2-Dv^2$, then the class polynomial $H_D$
splits modulo $r$, and its roots give $j$-invariants over $\mathbb F_r$ with
the desired CM.

[CITED] Theorem 1.4 gives an unconditional probabilistic class-group
enumeration algorithm polynomial in $|D|$ up to logarithmic factors, and
deterministic bounds under GRH.

[CONDITIONAL: floating-point approximations retain the required precision]
Corollary 1.3 computes a class polynomial in
$O(|D|\log^{6+\epsilon}|D|)$ time; the paper explicitly labels the combined
rounding/correctness conclusion heuristic.

## Assumptions and limits

[CITED] The height bound used to choose the target precision is rigorous, but
the paper does not give a full rounding-error proof for its sharp
floating-point complexity claim.
