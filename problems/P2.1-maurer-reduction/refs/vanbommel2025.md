# Van Bommel et al. 2025 - prescribed abelian-variety orders

**Source:** Raymond van Bommel, Edgar Costa, Bjorn Poonen, Alexander Smith,
and Wanlin Li, "Abelian Varieties of Prescribed Order over Finite Fields,"
*Mathematische Annalen* 392, 1167--1202, 2025.

**DOI:** <https://doi.org/10.1007/s00208-024-03084-4>

**Preprint:** <https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.13651>

**Checked results:**

- [CITED] Theorem 1.1 realizes every integer in a large central interval for
  fixed $q$ and sufficiently large dimension, even by a geometrically simple
  ordinary principally polarized variety.
- [CITED] Theorem 1.7 determines the asymptotic universally realizable central
  interval for fixed dimension $n\ge3$ as $q\to\infty$; Remark 1.9 extends
  the prime-field statement to $n=2$.
- [CITED] Remark 1.11 gives the ordinary dimension-two constant
  $4-2\sqrt2$.
- [CITED] Theorem 2.1 records the ordinary Honda--Tate polynomial criterion.

**Use here:** [CONDITIONAL: Riemann Hypothesis] For $n=2$, the realizable
interval has length $\Theta(r^{3/2})=\Theta((r^2)^{3/4})$, which lies inside
Younis's RH polylog-smooth short-interval range.

**Limitation:** [PROVED] The theorem establishes an abelian variety in an
isogeny class but does not supply the explicit genus-two Jacobian and strong
embedding needed for a uniform Maurer--Wolf reduction.
