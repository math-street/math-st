## Result used in P2.1

[CITED] Section 1.3 identifies a $d$-dimensional torus over a finite field
$k=\mathbb F_q$ with an integral rank-$d$ character lattice carrying the
finite-order Frobenius matrix $\Phi$.  Theorem 1.3.11 gives

$$
  |T(k)|=(-1)^d\det(\Phi-qI)=\det(qI-\Phi).
$$

[PROVED] Since $\Phi$ has finite order, its characteristic polynomial is a
product of cyclotomic polynomials.  Therefore every full finite-field torus
order is a product of cyclotomic values $\Phi_n(q)$, with total cyclotomic
degree equal to the torus dimension.
