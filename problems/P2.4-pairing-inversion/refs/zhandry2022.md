# Zhandry 2022

Mark Zhandry, “To Label, or Not To Label (in Generic Groups),” CRYPTO 2022, LNCS 13509, 66–96, doi:10.1007/978-3-031-15982-4_3.

- [CITED] The paper distinguishes Shoup's random-representation model from Maurer's pointer/hidden-state model and formalizes the related type-safe model.
- [CITED] Theorems 1.5 and 1.6 establish matching security implications for single-stage games that exist in both models.
- [CITED] The paper warns that model names cannot be interchanged silently, especially for multi-stage cryptosystems and representation-dependent operations.
- [PROVED] P2.4 FAPI-1 is a single-stage typed search game; A002 now names RR/Shoup as its theorem model and uses TS/Maurer only as a corroborating formulation.
