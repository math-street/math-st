# Maurer 2005

Ueli Maurer, “Abstract Models of Computation in Cryptography,” *Cryptography and Coding 2005*, LNCS 3796, 1–12.

- [CITED] Section 1.2 models every generic DLP value as an affine expression \(ax+b\) and bounds the chance of a collision among \(k\) values by \(\binom k2/q\), where \(q\) is the largest prime divisor of the group order.
- [CITED] The abstract model exposes only allowed operations and relations on hidden state, clarifying why a typed target element cannot be supplied to a source-only DLP operation.
- [PROVED] A002 uses the same affine-collision invariant but states its final theorem directly in Shoup's random-representation model.
