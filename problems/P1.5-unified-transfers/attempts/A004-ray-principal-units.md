# A004 - Preserve point orientation in ray-class principal units

## Idea

- [CONJECTURE] Replacing an ordinary class group by a ray class group of
  modulus divisible by $r^2$ may expose an additive order-$r$ quotient that
  can receive an oriented point; a uniform nonzero evaluator satisfying SG-01
  would refute the current negative assessment.

## Prior art

- [CITED] Milne's ray-class exact sequence identifies the congruence part with
  $(\mathcal O_K/\mathfrak m)^\times$ modulo global units and gives its exact
  order (Milne 1997, Theorem V.1.5).

## Plan

- [PROVED] Compute the exact orders for $K=\mathbb Q(i)$ at moduli $r\mathcal
  O_K$ and $r^2\mathcal O_K$.
- [PROVED] Isolate and linearize the principal units, then identify exactly
  what data a source evaluator would still need.

## Execution log

- [EMPIRICAL: odd primes r in {3,5,7,13} and source primes 5 <= r <= 113]
  `code/probe_cm_class_targets.py` checked the ray-order formulas and every
  discrete logarithm in the real order-$r$ principal-unit line modulo $r^2$.

## Outcome

- [PROVED] Since $h(\mathbb Z[i])=1$ and its four units have trivial
  intersection with $1+r\mathbb Z[i]$ for odd $r$, Milne's formula gives
  $$|\operatorname{Cl}_{r}(\mathbb Q(i))|=
  \begin{cases}(r-1)^2/4,&r\equiv1\pmod4,\\(r^2-1)/4,&r\equiv3\pmod4.
  \end{cases}$$
  Thus $r$ does not divide the modulus-$r$ ray class number.
- [PROVED] At modulus $r^2$ the corresponding orders are
  $r^2(r-1)^2/4$ and $r^2(r^2-1)/4$, so their $r$-adic valuation is exactly 2.
- [PROVED] Multiplication induces an isomorphism
  $$(1+r\mathbb Z[i])/(1+r^2\mathbb Z[i])
    \longrightarrow (\mathbb Z[i]/r\mathbb Z[i],+),\qquad
    1+rz\longmapsto z,$$
  because $(1+rx)(1+ry)\equiv1+r(x+y)\pmod{r^2}$.
- [PROVED] The real line generated by $1+r$ is therefore a target cyclic group
  of order $r$ with constant-time logarithm
  $1+rn\mapsto n\bmod r$ once its residue representative is supplied.
- [PROVED] Defining $\phi(nP)=1+rn\pmod{r^2}$ is circular: evaluating the
  right-hand side from the ordinary point $nP$ requires precisely its source
  discrete logarithm.
- [PROVED] A cyclic $r^2$-level lift can be modeled by
  $\mathbb Z/r^2\mathbb Z$, with multiplication by $r$ sending a lift $x$ to
  its order-$r$ point $rx$.  The principal units send
  $x\mapsto(1+ra)x$ and preserve $rx$, so their $r$-element orbit consists of
  distinct lifts of one source point, not different multiples of that point.
- [PROVED] The full unit group changes a generator $P$ to $nP$ through
  $n\in(\mathbb Z/r\mathbb Z)^\times$; this action is multiplicative, has
  order $r-1$, omits the identity point, and is not a homomorphism from the
  additive order-$r$ source.
- [EMPIRICAL: odd primes 3 <= r <= 113] The level-module check in
  `code/probe_cm_class_targets.py` found $r$ principal-unit lifts above exactly
  one source point and $r-1$ nonzero source generators in the full-unit orbit.

## Post-mortem

**Why it failed:** [PROVED] Principal units encode motion inside the fibre of
the multiplication-by-$r$ map on level lifts, whereas source scalar
multiplication moves between fibres; the full ray-level rescaling action is
multiplicative rather than additive.

**What transfers:** [PROVED] Finding order-$r$ torsion in a target is not
enough: its geometric action must move the source point in the same direction
as the source group law.

- [PROVED] A022 later sharpens the computational obstruction: because the
  principal-unit logarithm is explicit, any nonzero point evaluator into this
  target recovers the source scalar with one call and is polynomial-time
  equivalent to source DLP.

**Would it work under different assumptions?** [CONJECTURE] A construction
that couples an $r^2$-lift fibre coordinate to point addition, rather than to
level rescaling, could work; an explicit evaluator is a concrete refutation,
while the standard ray action does not provide one.
