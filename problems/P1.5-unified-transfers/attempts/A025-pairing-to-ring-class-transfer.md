# A025 - Pairing values as ordinary ring classes

## Result

- [PROVED] P1.5/Q004's former literal checklist has a positive transfer
  package under the same
  finite-field-DLP convention already used for the MOV/Frey--Ruck transfer.
  The target is the ordinary proper ideal class group
  $\operatorname{Pic}(\mathbb Z+p\mathbb Z[i])$, not a ray class group.
- [PROVED] The construction is not a fourth transfer mechanism. It is the
  established embedding-degree-two pairing transfer followed by the conductor
  exact-sequence presentation of a quadratic ring class group.
- [PROVED] The explicit output attached to a pairing value is a canonical
  reduced primitive positive-definite binary quadratic form of one fixed
  discriminant $-4p^2$.

## 1. Infinite uniform source family

Let $r$ range through the odd primes. By the Chinese remainder theorem, there
is a reduced residue class $a_r\bmod 4r$ satisfying

$$
a_r\equiv3\pmod4,\qquad a_r\equiv-1\pmod r.
$$

- [CITED] Linnik's theorem, with Xylouris's admissible exponent $5.2$, gives
  an effectively bounded prime
  $$
  p\equiv a_r\pmod{4r},\qquad p\le C(4r)^{5.2}
  $$
  for an absolute effective constant $C$.
- [PROVED] Hence $p\equiv3\pmod4$, $r\mid p+1$, and
  $\log p=O(\log r)$. The family of valid tuples $(p,r)$ is infinite,
  polynomially recognizable once the tuple is supplied, and has full input
  length $L=O(\log p)=O(\log r)$.
- [PROVED] Put
  $$
  E_p:\ y^2=x^3+x
  $$
  over $\mathbb F_p$. This is the standard trace-zero supersingular
  $j=1728$ curve for $p\equiv3\pmod4$, so
  $\#E_p(\mathbb F_p)=p+1$. Therefore it has a point $P$ of order $r$.
  Given $(p,r)$, projecting a random point by the cofactor $(p+1)/r$
  produces such a $P$ in expected polynomial time; alternatively $P$ is part
  of the ordinary source instance.

This use of Linnik proves a succinct infinite family. It does not claim a
deterministic polynomial-time algorithm that finds the least suitable $p$
from $r$ alone; SG-01 takes $(p,E,P,r)$ as the instance, and the transfer
setup and evaluator below are uniform polynomial-time algorithms on every
valid instance.

## 2. Nondegenerate pairing character

Write $\mathbb F_{p^2}=\mathbb F_p(i)$ with $i^2=-1$ and define

$$
\psi(x,y)=(-x,iy).
$$

- [CITED] This is the standard distortion map on the $j=1728$ trace-zero
  curve. Frobenius fixes $P$ and sends $\psi(P)$ to $-\psi(P)$, so the two
  points lie in the distinct Frobenius eigenspaces and are independent in
  $E_p[r]$.
- [CITED] Let $e_r$ be the Weil pairing, or equivalently use the checked
  nondegenerate reduced Tate pairing. For
  $T=\psi(P)$, the value
  $$
  \zeta=e_r(P,T)
  $$
  has exact order $r$ in $\mu_r\subset\mathbb F_{p^2}^{\times}$.
- [PROVED] Therefore
  $$
  z:\langle P\rangle\longrightarrow\mu_r,\qquad
  z(Q)=e_r(Q,T)
  $$
  is a uniform polynomial-time injective homomorphism.

## 3. The ordinary ring class target

Let $K=\mathbb Q(i)$, $\mathcal O_K=\mathbb Z[i]$, and

$$
\mathcal O_p=\mathbb Z+p\mathbb Z[i].
$$

This order has conductor $\mathfrak c=p\mathbb Z[i]$ and discriminant
$\Delta_p=-4p^2$.

- [CITED] The conductor exact sequence (Conrad, Theorem 5.3 and Example 5.4)
  specializes to
  $$
  1\longrightarrow
  \mathcal O_K^\times/\mathcal O_p^\times
  \longrightarrow
  (\mathcal O_K/\mathfrak c)^\times/
  (\mathcal O_p/\mathfrak c)^\times
  \mathop{\longrightarrow}^{\kappa}
  \operatorname{Pic}(\mathcal O_p)
  \longrightarrow1, \tag{A025.1}
  $$
  because $\operatorname{Pic}(\mathbb Z[i])=1$.
- [PROVED] Since $p\equiv3\pmod4$,
  $$
  \mathcal O_K/\mathfrak c\cong\mathbb F_{p^2},\qquad
  \mathcal O_p/\mathfrak c\cong\mathbb F_p,
  $$
  and $\mathcal O_K^\times/\mathcal O_p^\times$ is generated by the class of
  $i$ and has order two. Thus
  $$
  \operatorname{Pic}(\mathcal O_p)
  \cong
  \mathbb F_{p^2}^{\times}/
  \bigl(\mathbb F_p^\times\langle i\rangle\bigr),\qquad
  h(-4p^2)=\frac{p+1}{2}. \tag{A025.2}
  $$
- [PROVED] The map in (A025.1) is explicitly
  $$
  \kappa(\alpha)=
  [\,\alpha\mathbb Z[i]\cap\mathcal O_p\,]. \tag{A025.3}
  $$
  Because
  $$
  \gcd\bigl(r,\,|\mathbb F_p^\times\langle i\rangle|\bigr)
  =\gcd(r,2(p-1))=1,
  $$
  the restriction of $\kappa$ to $\mu_r$ is injective.

Consequently the desired transfer is

$$
\phi=\kappa\circ z:
\langle P\rangle\longrightarrow\operatorname{Pic}(\mathcal O_p).
\tag{A025.4}
$$

It is a nonzero homomorphism and hence injective.

## 4. Explicit canonical quadratic-form evaluator

Let $z(Q)=a+bi\in\mathbb F_{p^2}^\times$. Multiplication by
$\mathbb F_p^\times$ does not change its class in (A025.2).

- [PROVED] If $a\ne0$, put
  $$
  t=ba^{-1}\pmod p,\qquad 0\le t<p.
  $$
  With $\alpha=1+ti$, direct solution of
  $tx+y\equiv0\pmod p$ gives
  $$
  \alpha\mathbb Z[i]\cap\mathcal O_p
  =
  [\,1+t^2,\,-pt+pi\,]. \tag{A025.5}
  $$
  Under the convention
  $[A,(-B+\sqrt{\Delta_p})/2]$, this is the primitive form
  $$
  F_t=(1+t^2,\ 2pt,\ p^2),\qquad
  \operatorname{disc}(F_t)=-4p^2. \tag{A025.6}
  $$
- [PROVED] The coefficients in (A025.6) have $O(\log p)$ bits. Ordinary
  positive-form reduction returns the unique canonical reduced
  representative in polynomial time.
- [PROVED] If $a=0$, the projective residue is represented by the Gaussian
  unit $i$, so its class is principal. This branch cannot occur for a
  nonidentity element of the odd-order group $\mu_r$, but it makes the
  evaluator total.
- [PROVED] Miller pairing evaluation, one inversion in $\mathbb F_p$, forming
  (A025.6), and Gauss reduction all take time polynomial in
  $L=O(\log p)$.

This is a direct finite-field-coordinate-to-ordinary-form evaluator. It is
outside A024's VFB model because the lifted residue $t$ is copied into a form
coefficient; the exact sequence supplies the homomorphism invariant that A024
deliberately lacked.

## 5. Target discrete logarithm

There are two compatible target routes.

### 5.1 Reduction back to the finite-field torus

Let $(A,B,C)$ be a primitive form of discriminant $-4p^2$. Then $p\nmid A$:
if $p\mid A$, the discriminant equation forces $p\mid B,C$, contradicting
primitivity. Its ideal

$$
J=[\,A,-B/2+pi\,]
$$

is therefore prime to the conductor.

- [PROVED] Extend $J$ to the Euclidean domain $\mathbb Z[i]$ and compute
  $$
  \gamma=\gcd_{\mathbb Z[i]}(A,-B/2+pi).
  $$
  Extension and contraction give
  $J=\gamma\mathbb Z[i]\cap\mathcal O_p$. The Gaussian Euclidean algorithm is
  polynomial in $\log p$.
- [PROVED] The value
  $$
  \tau([J])=(\gamma\bmod p)^{\,2(p-1)}
  \in\mathbb F_{p^2}^{\times} \tag{A025.7}
  $$
  is independent of the generator and ideal representative: an
  $\mathbb F_p^\times$ factor is killed by $p-1$, and a Gaussian-unit factor
  is killed by $2(p-1)$.
- [PROVED] On $\kappa(\mu_r)$, formula (A025.7) is exponentiation by
  $2(p-1)\equiv-4\pmod r$. Since $r$ is odd, this exponent is invertible
  modulo $r$. Thus $\tau$ is injective on the transfer image and preserves
  discrete logarithms:
  $$
  \tau(\phi(Q))=\tau(\phi(P))^x
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad Q=xP. \tag{A025.8}
  $$

The target DLP is therefore polynomial-time reducible to the same
degree-two finite-field DLP used by the original pairing transfer.

### 5.2 End-to-end asymptotics

- [CITED] Under the finite-field NFS convention already used for the
  MOV/Frey--Ruck branch, the target cost is
  $L_{p^2}[1/3,O(1)]=\exp(o(\log r))$, because Linnik gives
  $\log p=O(\log r)$.
- [CONDITIONAL: ERH and the represented-input factor-base hypothesis] The
  independent Hafner--McCurley route also gives
  $$
  \exp\!\left(
    O\!\left(\sqrt{\log|\Delta_p|\log\log|\Delta_p|}\right)
  \right)
  =\exp(o(\log r)).
  $$
- [PROVED] With $n=\lceil\log_2r\rceil$,
  $$
  \log_2|\Delta_p|=2\log_2p+2=\Theta(n),
  $$
  along the Linnik family. Hence the target lies inside the SG-25 window
  $$
  2n-O(\log n)
  \le\log_2|\Delta_p|
  =o(n^2/\log n).
  $$
