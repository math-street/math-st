# Moody 2008/2009

- [CITED] Dustin Moody, "The Diffie--Hellman Problem and Generalization of
  Verheul's Theorem," IACR ePrint 2008/456; *Designs, Codes and
  Cryptography* 52(3), 381--390, 2009.
- [CITED] Moody generalizes the Verheul consequence to arbitrary finite
  fields and associated supersingular curves: for the constructed curve, an
  efficiently computable isomorphism from $\mathbb F_q^\times$ to the
  relevant elliptic subgroup makes Diffie--Hellman efficiently solvable in
  both groups.
- [CITED] The proof again depends on a computable pairing and a computable
  distortion map. It supplies a template for auditing proposed reverse
  homomorphisms, not an unconditional prohibition on homomorphisms into an
  unrelated ordinary ideal-class group.

Primary source: <https://eprint.iacr.org/2008/456>.
