# Conrad - The conductor ideal of an order

- [CITED] Keith Conrad, "The Conductor Ideal of an Order," expository notes,
  accessed 2026-07-23.
- [CITED] The notes prove the exact sequence
  $$
  1\to\mathcal O_K^\times/\mathcal O^\times
  \to(\mathcal O_K/\mathfrak c)^\times/
       (\mathcal O/\mathfrak c)^\times
  \to\operatorname{Cl}(\mathcal O)
  \to\operatorname{Cl}(\mathcal O_K)\to1
  $$
  and identify the residue map as
  $\alpha\mapsto[\alpha\mathcal O_K\cap\mathcal O]$.
- [CITED] Example 5.4 treats
  $\mathcal O=\mathbb Z+p\mathbb Z[i]$ explicitly. For inert
  $p\equiv3\pmod4$, its class number is $(p+1)/2$.
- [PROVED] A025 combines this standard exact sequence with the existing
  embedding-degree-two pairing. The exact sequence itself is not a
  repository-original result.
- [PROVED] A029 specializes the same sequence at conductor \(r^2\), using
  \(1+ri\bmod r^2\) to give a uniform exact order-\(r\) ring-class target.
  The conductor theory remains prior art; A029's contribution is the
  SG-30 extraction, closed reduced form, and bit-complexity certificate.
