# Castagnos--Laguillaumie 2009

- [CITED] Guilhem Castagnos and Fabien Laguillaumie, "On the Security of
  Cryptosystems with Quadratic Decryption: The Nicest Cryptanalysis,"
  EUROCRYPT 2009, LNCS 5479, 260--277.
- [CITED] Lemma 1 gives the effective isomorphism
  \[
  (\mathcal O_K/f\mathcal O_K)^\times/
  (\mathbb Z/f\mathbb Z)^\times
  \;\widetilde{\longrightarrow}\;
  \ker\bigl(\operatorname{Cl}(\mathcal O_f)
  \longrightarrow\operatorname{Cl}(\mathcal O_K)\bigr)
  \]
  for general conductor \(f\) in the stated imaginary-quadratic setting.
  The paper also explains both directions: contraction maps a residue to a
  kernel class, while a reduced kernel ideal is made conductor-coprime,
  extended to \(\mathcal O_K\), principalized, and reduced modulo \(f\).
- [CITED] The paper uses this effective kernel description in its
  polynomial-time cryptanalysis of NICE.
- [PROVED] A026's proposed arbitrary-form conductor inverse is therefore
  prior art in general form, not a publication-novel theorem. The local
  additive/tame decomposition remains useful as an audit and control.
