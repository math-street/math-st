# Verheul 2001/2004

- [CITED] Eric R. Verheul, "Evidence that XTR Is More Secure than
  Supersingular Elliptic Curve Cryptosystems," EUROCRYPT 2001, LNCS 2045,
  195--210, doi:10.1007/3-540-44987-6_13; full version, *Journal of
  Cryptology* 17(4), 277--296, 2004,
  doi:10.1007/s00145-004-0313-x.
- [CITED] Verheul proves that an efficiently computable homomorphism from the
  XTR subgroup back to the relevant supersingular elliptic subgroup would
  make Diffie--Hellman efficiently solvable in both groups. The proof uses
  the existing MOV direction together with pairings/distortion structure.
- [PROVED] This is direct prior art for the strategy "an efficient reverse
  homomorphism has a hard-problem consequence." It is not a degree or branch
  lower bound and does not apply automatically to an ordinary number-field
  class group, where P1.5 has no checked efficiently computable nondegenerate
  pairing or distortion map with the required codomain.

Primary sources: [EUROCRYPT version](https://www.iacr.org/archive/eurocrypt2001/20450194.pdf)
and [journal full version](https://www.cs.ru.nl/E.Verheul/papers/Joc2004/joc2004.pdf).
