# Coppersmith--Shparlinski 2000

- [CITED] Don Coppersmith and Igor E. Shparlinski, "On Polynomial
  Approximation of the Discrete Logarithm and the Diffie--Hellman Mapping,"
  *Journal of Cryptology* 13(3), 339--360, 2000,
  doi:10.1007/s001450010002.
- [CITED] Theorem 1 proves that if a polynomial of degree $d$ agrees modulo
  $p$ with the discrete-logarithm function on an arbitrary
  $S\subseteq\mathbb F_p^\times$, then

  \[
  d\ge \frac{|S|(|S|-1)}{2(p-2)}.
  \]
  Its proof chooses a quotient with many representations in $S/S$, then
  zero-counts a multiplicative translate defect. This is the direct
  multiplicative analogue of the same-difference overlap mechanism in P1.5
  Theorem 4.1.
- [CITED] Theorem 3 treats a bivariate algebraic relation
  $F(x,\operatorname{ind}x)=0$ and obtains a total-degree bound of order
  $|S|/\sqrt p$. The later Diffie--Hellman results use character sums and
  solution-count estimates for specific finite-field encodings.
- [PROVED] These theorems concern polynomials or algebraic relations computing
  scalar discrete-logarithm/Diffie--Hellman functions in finite fields. They
  do not state a bound for a rational map from an elliptic curve to an
  arbitrary affine algebraic group through a fixed faithful representation.

Primary sources: <https://doi.org/10.1007/s001450010002> and the
