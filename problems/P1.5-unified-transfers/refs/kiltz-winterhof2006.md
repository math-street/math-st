# Kiltz--Winterhof 2006

- [CITED] Eike Kiltz and Arne Winterhof, "Polynomial Interpolation of
  Cryptographic Functions Related to Diffie--Hellman and Discrete Logarithm
  Problem," *Discrete Applied Mathematics* 154(2), 326--336, 2006,
  doi:10.1016/j.dam.2005.03.030.
- [CITED] The paper defines polynomially transformed Diffie--Hellman and
  discrete-logarithm functions, proves that sufficiently useful low-degree
  interpolants would give algorithms for the underlying hard problem, and
  proves degree lower bounds for those finite-field interpolation problems.
- [PROVED] Its domain, output encoding, and polynomial model are specialized
  to finite-field cryptographic functions. It does not provide a theorem for
  rational maps $E\dashrightarrow H$ with $H$ an arbitrary affine,
  proper, or mixed algebraic group, and it does not model lifts, valuations,
  or ordinary number-field ideal reduction.

Primary sources: <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.dam.2005.03.030> and the
[CWI publication record](https://ir.cwi.nl/pub/15510).
