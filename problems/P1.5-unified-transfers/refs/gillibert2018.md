# Gillibert 2018

- [CITED] Jean Gillibert, "From Picard groups of hyperelliptic curves to
  class groups of quadratic fields," arXiv:1807.02823, 2018.
- [CITED] For a curve \(C/\mathbb Q\), a fixed algebraic point \(P\), and an
  admissible degree-zero line bundle \(L\), the specialization
  \(L\mapsto P^*L\) is a homomorphism into the ideal class group of the number
  field \(\mathbb Q(P)\).
- [CITED] The paper proves nontrivial and unbounded-order specializations in
  imaginary quadratic fields for stated hyperelliptic families and explains
  the Buell--Soleng maps through the Mazur--Tate class group pairing.
- [PROVED] The construction varies an algebraic point over number fields and
  does not provide a cross-characteristic evaluator on finite-field points.
