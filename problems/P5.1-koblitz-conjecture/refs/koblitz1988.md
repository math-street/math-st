# Koblitz 1988 — prime orders of elliptic-curve reductions

**Reference:** Neal Koblitz, “Primality of the number of points on an elliptic curve over a finite field,” *Pacific Journal of Mathematics* 131(1), 157–165, 1988.

- [CITED] For a non-CM curve over $\mathbb{Q}$ not isogenous to a curve with nontrivial rational torsion, Koblitz conjectures a prime-order count asymptotic to $C_E x/(\log x)^2$. (Conjecture A.)
- [CITED] The original local factor compares the density of matrices in the mod-$\ell$ Galois image without eigenvalue 1 to the density $1-1/\ell$ for random integers. (Section 3.)
- [CITED] Koblitz reports measurements through $p<30000$ for three curves with full mod-$\ell$ images. (Table I.)
- [CITED] The paper treats CM curves separately because their division-field images and constants differ. (Section 4.)

**What it leaves open:**

- [CITED] No fixed positive-constant instance of the conjectured prime-order asymptotic is proved. (Koblitz 1988; status cross-checked against Zywina 2011, Section 9.)
