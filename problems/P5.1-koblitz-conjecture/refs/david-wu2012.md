# David–Wu 2012 — almost-prime orders and the GRH dependency

**Reference:** Chantal David and Jie Wu, “Almost prime values of the order of elliptic curves over finite fields,” *Forum Mathematicum* 24(1), 99–119, 2012; arXiv:0812.2860.

- [CITED] Their theta-hypothesis excludes zeros with real part greater than $\theta$ for the Dedekind zeta and relevant Artin $L$-functions of elliptic-curve division fields; GRH corresponds to $\theta=1/2$. (Hypothesis 3.4.)
- [CITED] Effective Chebotarev under this hypothesis controls the remainder when counting primes with Frobenius in divisibility classes for squarefree moduli. (Theorem 3.9.)
- [CITED] Those remainder estimates are inserted into the weighted sieve through equations (4.11)--(4.14), with level $D=x^{(2/5)(1-\theta)(1-\varepsilon)}$. (Section 4.)
- [CITED] The resulting lower bound has at most $r=\lfloor(18+2\theta)/(5(1-\theta))\rfloor+1$ prime factors; GRH gives $r=8$, and $\theta<11/21$ is already sufficient for the eight-factor corollary. (Theorem 1.1 and Corollary 1.2.)
- [CITED] The Selberg-sieve upper bound for prime orders has multiplier $5/(1-\theta)+\varepsilon$, giving $10+\varepsilon$ under GRH. (Theorem 1.3 and equation (5.2).)

**What it leaves open:**

- [CITED] The method supplies neither a prime-order lower bound nor the conjectured asymptotic for one fixed non-CM curve. (Sections 1, 4--5.)
