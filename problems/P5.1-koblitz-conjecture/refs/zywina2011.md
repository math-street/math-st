# Zywina 2011 — corrected Koblitz constants

**Reference:** David Zywina, “A refinement of Koblitz's conjecture,” *International Journal of Number Theory* 7(3), 739–769, 2011; arXiv:0909.5280.

- [CITED] The corrected constant is the divisibility-ordered limit of adelic Galois-image densities, so it captures correlations between distinct division fields and may vanish. (Definition 2.1 and Conjecture 2.2.)
- [CITED] Away from finitely many exceptional primes for a non-CM curve, the Euler factor is $1-(\ell^2-\ell-1)/((\ell-1)^3(\ell+1))$. (Proposition 2.4.)
- [CITED] The universal product of these factors is approximately $0.505166168239435774$. (Equation (2.3).)
- [CITED] The Serre curve $y^2=x^3+6x-2$ has correction factor $10/9$ and $C_{E,1}\approx0.561295742488261971$. (Section 5, equation (5.1).)
- [CITED] The refined finite-cutoff predictor is $C_{E,t}\sum_p(\log(p+1)-\log t)^{-1}$, equivalently its prime-number-theorem integral, and is expected to converge faster than $C_{E,t}x/(\log x)^2$. (Section 2.4.)
- [CITED] The paper's non-CM fixed-curve partial results are almost-prime lower bounds and prime-order upper bounds under GRH or weaker zero-free hypotheses; unconditional upper bounds lose a logarithmic factor. (Section 9.)
- [CITED] Proposition 2.4 reduces a non-CM quotient constant to one finite-level density at $t\prod_{\ell\mid tM}\ell$ and universal full-image factors away from $tM$. (Proposition 2.4.)
- [CITED] For the CM curve $y^2=x^3-x$ over $\mathbb Q(i)$, the relevant quotient is $t=8$, rational primes are restricted to $p\equiv1\pmod4$, and $C_{E_{\mathbb Q(i)},8}\approx1.067350894$. (Section 7, Lemma 7.1.)
- [CITED] The CM product can be evaluated absolutely convergently after extracting $L(1,\chi)^{-1}=4/\pi$. (End of the proof of Lemma 7.1.)

**What it leaves open:**

- [CITED] The positive-constant fixed-curve prime-order asymptotic remains unproved. (Section 9.)
