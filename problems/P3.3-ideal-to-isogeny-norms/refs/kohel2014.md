# Kohel--Lauter--Petit--Tignol 2014

**Reference:** David Kohel, Kristin Lauter, Christophe Petit, and Jean-Pierre
Tignol, "On the quaternion $\ell$-isogeny path problem," *LMS Journal of
Computation and Mathematics* 17(A), 418--432, 2014,
doi:10.1112/S1461157014000151.

## Results in repository notation

[CITED] For prime $p\equiv3\pmod4$, the algebra
$B=\mathbb Q\langle i,j\rangle$ with $i^2=-1$, $j^2=-p$, and $ij=-ji=k$ is
ramified only at $p$ and infinity. One of the two maximal orders containing
$\mathbb Z\langle i,j\rangle$ is generated by
$i,(1+j)/2,(1+k)/2$; this is the order used in `lib/quaternion.py`.
[Lemma 2]

[CITED] For an integral left ideal $I$, the normalized form is
$q_I(x)=\operatorname{nrd}(x)/N(I)$, where
$N(I)=\sqrt{|\mathcal O/I|}$. If $x\in I$, then
$J=I\bar x/N(I)$ is an equivalent integral ideal with $N(J)=q_I(x)$.
[Section 2.4, Lemma 5]

[CITED] Under the paper's optimistic distribution heuristics, the complete
basic $\ell$-power ideal algorithm has expected polynomial time and produces
an ideal whose norm is approximately $p^{7/2}$ when its prime-norm input is
near $p^{1/2}$. The intermediate lifted quaternion has norm scale $p^3$.
[Theorem 7 and Sections 4.4--4.5]

## Assumptions

[CITED] The runtime and output-size estimates use heuristic randomness of
integers represented by the relevant quadratic forms and heuristic prime
distributions. [Theorem 7]

## What it rules out

[PROVED] The supplied P3.3 prompt's phrase "KLPT produces ideals around
$p^3$" does not match the cited paper's complete-algorithm estimate: $p^3$
describes the lifted element, while the final ideal estimate is $p^{7/2}$.
This is a distinction between two quantities, not a reproduction of either
experiment.

## What it leaves open

[CITED] The paper reports a separate prime-norm representative procedure whose
outputs were only slightly larger than $p^{1/2}$, but the complete prescribed
$\ell$-power construction is much larger. [Sections 3.1, 4.5, Appendix A.1]
