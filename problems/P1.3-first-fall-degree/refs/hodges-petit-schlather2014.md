# Hodges, Petit, and Schlather (2014)

Timothy J. Hodges, Christophe Petit, and Jacob Schlather, “First fall degree
and Weil descent,” *Finite Fields and Their Applications* 30 (2014), 155–177.
DOI: 10.1016/j.ffa.2014.07.001.

## Main result in repository notation

[CITED] The paper defines first fall degree through nontrivial relations in the
associated graded algebra of the finite-field function ring and proves general
upper bounds for systems obtained by Weil descent.

## Assumptions and relevance

[CITED] The paper treats first fall degree as a proxy for Gröbner termination;
Section 6.3 explicitly describes closeness to the degree reached by the
algorithm as a heuristic assumption.

## What it leaves open

[CITED] The conclusion asks for a quantitative connection between first fall
degree and termination degree. It does not prove that connection for Semaev
systems.

## Verification performed here

[CITED] Definitions 2.1–2.2 and Sections 6.2–6.3 were checked in the publisher's
PDF. No numerical table from this paper has yet been reproduced.
