# Kousidis and Wiemers (2019)

Stavros Kousidis and Andreas Wiemers, “On the first fall degree of summation
polynomials,” *Journal of Mathematical Cryptology* 13(3–4) (2019), 229–237.
DOI: 10.1515/jmc-2017-0022; arXiv:1906.05594.

## Results used here

[CITED] Theorem 3.2 proves the upper bound
$d_{\mathrm{ff}}\le m^2-m+1$ for the paper's binary Weil descent of
$S_{m+1}$, under its factor-base and nonzero-constant hypotheses.

[CITED] Section 4 defines its empirical first fall as the first Magma step
that adds a lower-degree polynomial and its empirical “degree of regularity”
as the highest Magma step degree. P1.3 records the latter as an algorithm
trace, reserving $d_{\mathrm{reg}}$ for the Hilbert-function invariant.

[CITED] Table 1 reports binary experiments with extension degrees larger than
the $n=2,3$ odd-characteristic range measured locally. It is therefore not a
like-for-like regression target.

## Boundary of the citation

[CITED] The paper asks whether the reported step degree is
$m^2-m+1+o(1)$ in its setting; its first-fall bound alone is not an
unconditional solving-degree bound.
