# Petit and Quisquater (2012)

Christophe Petit and Jean-Jacques Quisquater, “On polynomial systems arising
from a Weil descent,” in *Advances in Cryptology — ASIACRYPT 2012*, LNCS 7658,
451–466. DOI: 10.1007/978-3-642-34961-4_28. IACR ePrint 2012/146.

## Main relevance

[CONDITIONAL: the first-fall-degree assumption] The paper's subexponential
complexity estimate for binary-field ECDLP uses the assumption that degrees of
regularity of the relevant Weil-descent systems are only slightly larger than
their first fall degrees.

## What it leaves open

[CITED] The conclusion identifies rigorous analysis of the solving behavior
and wider experimental validation as open work.

## Verification performed here

[CITED] The abstract, complexity statement, and conclusion were checked in
IACR ePrint 2012/146. No computational result from the paper has yet been
reproduced locally.
