# Caminata and Gorla (2021) - solving degree and homogenization

**Source:** Alessio Caminata and Elisa Gorla, "Solving multivariate polynomial
systems and an invariant from commutative algebra," PQCrypto 2021, LNCS
12542, 3-36; arXiv:1706.06319v7.

**Checked:** 2026-07-01 against the final arXiv TeX source.

[CITED] Theorem 3.23 bounds DRL solving degree by the
Castelnuovo-Mumford regularity of the ideal generated by the homogenized input
under the stated zero-dimensional/generic-coordinate hypotheses.

[CITED] The theorem following the Macaulay-bound corollary proves that systems
containing all field equations are in generic coordinates. This does not say
that the raw homogenized input ideal is $t$-saturated.

[CITED] Remark 4.6 explains that equality between the ideal generated by the
homogenized inputs and the homogenization of the affine ideal is special; it
is equivalent to the inputs forming a Macaulay basis.

[PROVED] The representative quadratic Semaev system checked locally is not
raw $t$-saturated: saturation adds low-degree equations. Therefore the final
quadratic proof uses Salizzoni's closed-space bound rather than assuming raw
saturation. The exact elimination certificate is generated by
`../code/certify_quadratic_family.py`.
